# Crystal & Bee Shrimp (Caridina)

> The premium tier. Higher value, higher stakes, higher reward. For keepers who've mastered Neocaridina and want a challenge.

## Overview

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Species | Caridina cantonensis (and related) |
| Native range | Southern China, Hong Kong |
| Max size | 2.5-3 cm |
| Lifespan | 1.5-2 years |
| Temperature | 18-24°C (64-75°F) - COOLER than Neocaridina |
| pH | 5.8-6.8 (acidic, soft water) |
| Breeding | Moderate - requires stable soft/acid conditions |
| Difficulty | Intermediate to Advanced |

Caridina require **soft, acidic water** - the opposite of most tap water. This means RO water, buffering substrate, and more equipment. But the payoff is premium shrimp worth $5-100+ each.

## Water Parameters (CRITICAL)

**These are tighter ranges than Neocaridina. Precision matters here.**

| Parameter | Optimal | Acceptable | DANGER |
|-----------|---------|------------|--------|
| Temperature | 20-23°C (68-73°F) | 18-25°C | >27°C (lethal), <16°C |
| pH | 6.0-6.5 | 5.8-6.8 | >7.2 (wrong water type) |
| GH | 4-6 dGH | 3-8 dGH | <2 or >10 |
| KH | 0-1 dKH | 0-2 dKH | >3 (substrate will exhaust faster) |
| TDS | 130-180 | 100-220 | >250 |
| Ammonia | 0 | 0 | Any amount |
| Nitrite | 0 | 0 | Any amount |
| Nitrate | <5 mg/L | <10 mg/L | >20 mg/L |
| Copper | 0 | 0 | **LETHAL** |

**Key differences from Neocaridina**:
- Lower pH (acidic vs neutral)
- Much lower KH (0-1 vs 2-6)
- Lower TDS
- Cooler temperature
- Less tolerance for parameter swings

## Tank Setup

### Buffering Substrate (MANDATORY)

Active/buffering substrates lower and stabilize pH. Without one, you cannot keep Caridina.

| Substrate | pH Target | Lifespan | Cost | Notes |
|-----------|----------|----------|------|-------|
| ADA Amazonia Ver.2 | 6.0-6.5 | 12-18 months | $25-40/9L | The gold standard. Leeches ammonia initially (cycle 4-6 weeks) |
| UNS Controsoil | 6.2-6.5 | 12-18 months | $15-25/5L | Good alternative, less ammonia leaching |
| Brightwell Aquatics FlorinVolcanit | 6.0-6.5 | 12-18 months | $20-30 | Volcanic substrate |
| SL-Aqua Soil | 6.0-6.5 | 12-18 months | $20-30 | Designed for shrimp |
| Tropica Aquarium Soil | 6.0-6.5 | 12-18 months | $20-30 | European brand |

**Substrate exhaustion**: After 12-18 months, the substrate stops buffering and pH creeps up. At that point, you must either:
1. Replace the substrate (tear down and rebuild)
2. Add a thin layer of new substrate on top
3. Use a pH controller with CO2

### RO Water Setup (MANDATORY)

Tap water has too many minerals and too high KH for Caridina. You need **RO (Reverse Osmosis) water**.

| RO System | GPD | Cost | Notes |
|-----------|-----|------|-------|
| Budget countertop (4-stage) | 50-100 | $50-100 | Adequate for a few tanks |
| Under-sink (4-5 stage) | 75-150 | $100-200 | More convenient |
| RO Buddie (portable) | 50 | $50-60 | Popular in shrimp hobby |

**Remineralization**: RO water is pure (TDS ~0). You MUST add minerals back.

| Product | Target | Cost |
|---------|--------|------|
| **Salty Shrimp GH+** | GH 5-6, TDS 130-160 | $15-25 |
| **SL-Aqua GH Conditioner** | Similar | $15-20 |
| **Brightwell Shrimp Remineraliz** | Similar | $12-18 |

**Process**: Mix RO water + GH+ to target TDS (e.g., 150) -> verify GH (5-6) -> temperature match -> add to tank.

**No KH+ product** - Caridina need KH 0-1. The substrate handles pH buffering. Do NOT use GH/KH+ (that's for Neocaridina).

### Cycling a Caridina Tank

1. Set up tank with substrate, filter, plants
2. Fill with remineralized RO water
3. ADA Amazonia will leach ammonia for 2-4 weeks (this is normal and helps cycle)
4. **Wait 4-6 weeks minimum** before adding shrimp
5. Test: Ammonia 0, Nitrite 0, Nitrate <10, pH stable at 6.0-6.5
6. TDS stable at target
7. Then and only then: add shrimp

## Breeding

### Differences from Neocaridina

| Factor | Neocaridina | Caridina |
|--------|------------|---------|
| Breeding difficulty | Very easy | Moderate |
| Clutch size | 25-40 eggs | 15-25 eggs |
| Gestation | 28-35 days | 28-35 days |
| Colony growth rate | Doubles every 3-4 months | Doubles every 5-7 months |
| Parameter sensitivity | Tolerant | Strict |
| Babies survive | Almost all | Most (if parameters stable) |
| Selective breeding impact | Moderate | High (complex genetics) |

### Taiwan Bee Genetics (Simplified)

Taiwan Bee traits are **recessive**. This means:
- Crystal x Crystal = Crystal offspring
- Taiwan Bee x Taiwan Bee = Taiwan Bee offspring
- Crystal x Taiwan Bee = Crystal-looking offspring **carrying Taiwan Bee genes**
- Those carriers x carriers = 25% Taiwan Bee, 50% carriers, 25% pure Crystal

This is why Taiwan Bee shrimp command premium prices - you can't just buy Crystals and breed them into Taiwan Bees easily.

### Selective Breeding Strategy

1. Start with the best grade you can afford
2. Cull (remove from breeding tank) any offspring that don't meet your standard
3. Keep separate tanks for different breeding projects
4. Track lineage if possible (especially for Taiwan Bee)
5. Introduce new bloodlines every 8-12 months to prevent inbreeding

## Common Problems

| Problem | Cause | Solution |
|---------|-------|----------|
| pH creeping up | Substrate exhaustion | Test monthly. Replace substrate when pH won't hold <6.8 |
| Deaths after water change | TDS/temp shock | Match TDS within 10, temp within 1°C, drip new water in slowly |
| No breeding | Parameters off, stress, too few | Verify all params, ensure 3+ females, check food/biofilm |
| Bacterial infection | Poor water quality | Maintain pristine conditions, Indian almond leaves help |
| Summer deaths | Temperature too high (>26°C) | Aquarium fan, keep in coolest room, consider chiller for serious breeding |
| White ring of death | Molt failure, mineral deficiency | Verify GH 4-6, use mineral food weekly |
| TDS climbing | Evaporation concentrating minerals | Top off with pure RO water (not remineralized) |
| Babies dying | Fluctuating parameters, no biofilm | Stabilize params, add BacterAE, mature the tank longer |

### The Golden Rule of Caridina

**Do less.** The biggest killer of Caridina is keepers fiddling too much. Set up properly, cycle thoroughly, add shrimp, then **leave the tank alone** as much as possible. Small weekly water changes (10%), feed sparingly, test weekly, and resist the urge to fix what isn't broken.
