# Giant Freshwater Prawn (Macrobrachium rosenbergii)

> True freshwater. No salt needed. Grows to the size of a small lobster. The alternative for people who don't want to deal with salinity.

## Overview

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Native range | Indo-Pacific (India to Southeast Asia, Northern Australia) |
| Max size | 30+ cm, 300g+ (males) |
| Market size | 30-60g (6-9 months) |
| Growth rate | 0.5-1 g/week (slower than vannamei) |
| Salinity tolerance | 0-5 ppt (grow-out in freshwater) |
| Temperature range | 24-33°C (75-91°F) |
| Lifespan | 2-3 years |
| Diet | Omnivorous, scavenger, mildly predatory |

### Why Choose Rosenbergii Over Vannamei?

- **No salt** - true freshwater, no marine salt needed
- **Bigger animals** - impressive size at harvest
- **Higher per-animal value** - premium product
- **Hardier** in some ways - less disease-prone
- **Simpler water chemistry** - no salinity management

### Why Not?

- **Slower** - 6-9 months vs 3-4 months for vannamei
- **Territorial** - males fight, need hiding spots
- **Heterogeneous growth** - dominant males grow huge, subordinates stay small
- **Cannibalistic** - especially during molting
- **Larval rearing is complex** - most people buy juveniles

## Water Parameters

| Parameter | Optimal | Acceptable | Critical |
|-----------|---------|------------|----------|
| Temperature | 28-31°C (82-88°F) | 24-33°C | <20°C (stops feeding) |
| pH | 7.0-8.5 | 6.5-9.0 | <6.0 |
| Dissolved Oxygen | >5 mg/L | >3 mg/L | <2 mg/L |
| Ammonia (NH3) | <0.1 mg/L | <0.3 mg/L | >1 mg/L |
| Nitrite | <0.3 mg/L | <0.5 mg/L | >2 mg/L |
| Nitrate | <20 mg/L | <40 mg/L | >80 mg/L |
| Alkalinity | 30-60 mg/L CaCO3 | 20-150 mg/L | <10 mg/L |
| Hardness (GH) | 50-150 mg/L | 30-300 mg/L | <20 mg/L |

Simpler than vannamei - no salinity, less mineral fussiness.

## Tank / Pond Setup

### Key Differences from Vannamei

Rosenbergii are **bottom-dwellers that walk**. They need:

1. **Substrate** - sand or fine gravel (they walk and forage on the bottom)
2. **Shelters** - CRITICAL. PVC pipes, mesh bundles, stacked tiles, artificial plants
3. **More space per animal** - territorial, need room to establish territories
4. **Shallower water OK** - 30-50 cm depth is fine (they use the bottom, not the column)

### Container Options

| Setup | Volume | Stocking | Cost | Notes |
|-------|--------|----------|------|-------|
| Stock tank (300 gal) | 1135L | 20-40 prawns | $200-350 | Good starter |
| Lined pond (4x8 ft) | ~1000L | 30-50 prawns | $200-400 | Outdoor, seasonal |
| IBC tote | 1040L | 15-30 prawns | $50-150 | Works but square corners waste space |
| In-ground pond | Custom | 5-15/m² | $500+ | Best for serious production |

### Shelter Design

For every 10 prawns, provide at least:
- 10 PVC pipe sections (2-3" diameter, 6-8" long)
- Or: bundled mesh rolls, stacked clay pots, brick piles
- Shelters reduce cannibalism by 50%+
- Arrange in clusters with spaces between

## Stocking Density

| System | Density | Notes |
|--------|---------|-------|
| Pond (extensive) | 5-8/m² | Lowest management, best growth |
| Pond (semi-intensive) | 8-15/m² | More management, more yield |
| Tank (backyard) | 10-20/m² of bottom area | Need lots of shelters |
| Tank (intensive) | 20-30/m² | Expert level, high management |

**Calculate by bottom area**, not water volume. A 300-gallon stock tank with ~1 m² of bottom area holds 10-20 prawns comfortably.

## Male Morphotypes

This is unique to rosenbergii and critical to understand for farming.

### The Three Male Types

| Morphotype | Appearance | Behavior | Growth | % of Males |
|------------|-----------|----------|--------|------------|
| **Blue Claw (BC)** | Large blue claws, orange-brown body | Dominant, territorial, aggressive | Fastest growing, largest | 10-20% |
| **Orange Claw (OC)** | Orange claws, smaller | Subordinate to BC, sneaker maters | Medium growth | 30-40% |
| **Small Male (SM)** | Small claws, translucent | Non-territorial, sneaker | Slowest growth | 40-50% |

### What This Means for Farming

- **Blue Claw males** suppress growth of other males around them
- A tank with one BC male + many females grows best overall
- **Size grading** every 4-6 weeks improves average growth
- Harvest large BC males first (selective harvest)
- All-female culture is researched but not practical for backyard

## The Grow-Out Cycle

### Sourcing Juveniles

Larval rearing of rosenbergii is **complex** (requires 12 ppt brackish water, live Artemia feeding, 11 larval stages over 30-45 days). Most backyard farmers **buy juveniles** (1-3g, 60+ days old).

**Sources (USA)**:
- Kentucky State University Aquaculture Program
- State aquaculture extension programs
- Some private hatcheries (seasonal availability)
- Availability is more limited than vannamei PLs

### Timeline

| Month | Stage | Avg Weight | Activity |
|-------|-------|-----------|----------|
| 1 | Juvenile | 1-5g | Stock, establish territories, frequent feeding |
| 2-3 | Growing | 5-15g | Growth phase, size grading at month 2 |
| 4-5 | Sub-adult | 15-30g | Males differentiating, selective harvest begins |
| 6-7 | Adult | 30-60g | Selective harvest of large males |
| 8-9 | Mature | 40-100g+ | Final harvest, some BC males very large |

### Size Grading

Every 4-6 weeks, sort prawns by size:
1. Drain tank partially
2. Net out all prawns
3. Separate into 2-3 size groups
4. Re-stock in separate containers or sections
5. Harvest the largest

This prevents dominant males from suppressing subordinate growth.

## Feeding

### Diet

Rosenbergii are **omnivorous scavengers**. They eat:
- Commercial pellet feed (25-35% protein, sinking pellets)
- Dead plant matter, detritus
- Small invertebrates, worms
- Other prawns (cannibalism during molting!)

### Feeding Schedule

| Stage | Feed Rate (%BW/day) | Protein % | Feedings/Day |
|-------|---------------------|-----------|-------------|
| Juvenile (<5g) | 8-10% | 35-40% | 4 |
| Growing (5-15g) | 5-7% | 30-35% | 3 |
| Sub-adult (15-30g) | 3-5% | 28-32% | 2 |
| Adult (>30g) | 2-3% | 25-30% | 2 |

- Use **sinking pellets** (they feed on the bottom)
- Feed in late afternoon/evening (they're more active at night)
- Spread feed across the tank bottom (not one spot - reduces fighting)
- Supplement with blanched vegetables, earthworms, fish scraps

## Breeding

### The Challenge

Unlike Neocaridina (which breed easily in captivity), rosenbergii larvae require:
- Brackish water (12 ppt) for larval development
- Live Artemia nauplii as first food
- 30-45 days of careful larval rearing
- Temperature control (28-30°C)
- 11 distinct larval stages before metamorphosis to post-larvae

### For Backyard Farmers

**Recommendation**: Buy juveniles. Don't attempt larval rearing unless you have experience with both freshwater and marine aquaculture. The larval stage failure rate for beginners is very high.

### If You Want to Try Breeding

1. Berried (egg-carrying) females are easy to produce - just keep males and females together
2. Females carry 10,000-50,000 eggs for 19-21 days
3. Larvae hatch and need immediate transfer to 12 ppt brackish water
4. Feed Artemia nauplii 4-6x daily
5. Maintain pristine water quality
6. Metamorphosis to PL at day 30-45
7. Gradually acclimate PL to freshwater

## Common Problems

| Problem | Cause | Solution |
|---------|-------|----------|
| Cannibalism | Molting vulnerability, overcrowding, no shelter | Add more shelters, reduce density, feed adequately |
| Stunted growth | Dominant male suppression, overcrowding | Size grade regularly, selective harvest |
| Fighting/injuries | Territorial males | More space, more shelters, reduce male:female ratio |
| Soft shell | Mineral deficiency | Check calcium, add crushed coral or calcium supplement |
| Deaths after molt | Cannibalism during soft-shell phase | Ensure abundant shelters, feed well before expected molts |
| Females not breeding | Temperature too low, immature | Maintain 28-30°C, wait for maturity (4-5 months) |
| Walking out of tank | Exploring (they're crawlers!) | Cover tank completely, seal any gaps |

**Important**: Rosenbergii CAN AND WILL walk out of tanks. They climb airline tubing, overflow pipes, and any wet surface. **Seal all openings.**

## Rosenbergii vs Vannamei: Side-by-Side

| Factor | L. vannamei | M. rosenbergii |
|--------|------------|----------------|
| Water type | Saltwater/brackish (can adapt to FW) | Freshwater |
| Time to market | 90-120 days | 180-270 days |
| Market size | 15-25g | 30-60g+ |
| Stocking density | 50-300/m³ | 5-20/m² |
| Growth rate | 1-1.5 g/week | 0.5-1 g/week |
| Salt needed | Yes (or mineral supplementation) | No |
| Cannibalism risk | Moderate | High |
| Disease risk | Higher (WSSV, EMS, etc.) | Lower |
| Shelter needed | Optional | Mandatory |
| Larval rearing | Commercial hatcheries available | Complex, limited availability |
| Flavor | Classic shrimp | Sweeter, lobster-like |
| Best for | Quick cycles, biofloc, indoor | Ponds, outdoor, patience |
| Difficulty | Moderate | Moderate-High |

**Choose vannamei** if you want fast turnover and are OK with salt.
**Choose rosenbergii** if you want freshwater simplicity and bigger animals, and can wait 6-9 months.
