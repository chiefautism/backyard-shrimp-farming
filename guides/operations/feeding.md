## Feed Tray Management

Feed trays are essential for monitoring actual consumption and preventing overfeeding.

### DIY Feed Tray

Materials: Window screen mesh + PVC pipe frame (12"x12")
Cost: ~$3 each
Place at tank bottom, add measured feed, check after 2 hours.

### Reading Feed Trays

| Tray Status After 2 Hours | Action |
|---------------------------|--------|
| Completely clean | Increase feed by 10-15% next feeding |
| Small amount remaining (<10%) | Perfect. Maintain current amount. |
| Significant food remaining (>25%) | Reduce feed by 20-30% |
| All food remaining | Something is wrong. Check water quality. Skip next feeding. |

### Recipe 1: High-Protein Grower (35% protein)

| Ingredient | Percentage | Purpose |
|-----------|-----------|---------|

| Astaxanthin | 1% | Color enhancement |
| Garlic powder | 1% | Immune stimulant, anti-Vibrio |

### Recipe 2: Ornamental Shrimp Food

| Ingredient | Percentage |
|-----------|-----------|
| Spirulina powder | 25% |
| Blanched spinach (dried, ground) | 20% |
| Fish meal (or shrimp meal) | 15% |
| Moringa leaf powder | 10% |
| Wheat flour | 10% |
| Calcium carbonate powder | 8% |
| Dried nettle leaf | 5% |
| Bee pollen | 4% |
| Montmorillonite clay | 3% |

### Preparation Method

1. **Grind** all dry ingredients to fine powder (coffee grinder or blender)
2. **Mix** dry ingredients thoroughly in bowl
3. **Add** fish oil and lecithin (if using)
4. **Add water** gradually until dough forms (not too wet - should hold shape)
5. **Press** through pasta maker (thin setting) or meat grinder (small die)
6. **Dry** on baking sheet in dehydrator at 150°F (65°C) for 4-6 hours OR oven at lowest setting with door cracked
7. **Break** dried sheets into pellet-sized pieces
8. **Store** in airtight container in refrigerator (keeps 2-3 months) or freezer (6+ months)

## Feed Conversion Ratio (FCR)

**FCR = Total Feed Used / Total Weight Gained**

| FCR | Rating | Notes |
|-----|--------|-------|
| 1.0-1.3 | Excellent | Commercial farm level |
| 1.3-1.6 | Good | Well-managed backyard |
| 1.6-2.0 | Average | Room for improvement |
| >2.0 | Poor | Overfeeding or poor feed quality |

### Improving FCR

1. Use feed trays (stop overfeeding)
2. Match pellet size to shrimp size
3. Feed more frequently in smaller amounts
4. Better feed quality = better conversion
5. Maintain optimal temperature (28-32°C for vannamei)
6. Healthy biofloc contributes 30-50% of nutrition (reduces feed needed)

## Common Feeding Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It's Bad | Fix |
|---------|-------------|-----|
| Overfeeding | Ammonia spike, low DO, disease | Use feed trays, weigh feed |
| Wrong pellet size | Small shrimp can't eat big pellets | Match size to growth stage |
| Feeding during stress | Uneaten food fouls water | Skip feeding after water changes, during molting events |
| No feed trays | Can't monitor consumption | Build DIY trays ($3 each) |
| Single daily feeding | Shrimp graze continuously | Split into 3-4 smaller feedings |
| Ignoring biofloc nutrition | Over-supplementing in biofloc | Reduce commercial feed by 30-50% in biofloc |
| Stale feed | Nutrient degradation, mold | Store sealed, cool, use within 3 months |
