## Postlarvae (PL) Suppliers -- SPF Vannamei

### Ordering Tips

- **Order early.** Peak demand is March-June. Hatcheries sell out.
- **Minimum orders** are typically 10,000-50,000 PL. Some suppliers will work with smaller quantities at a higher per-unit price. Coordinate with nearby growers to split large orders.
- **Shipping** is usually overnight air freight. PL arrive in oxygenated bags inside insulated boxes. Have your nursery tank ready and acclimated before PL arrive.
- **Request documentation:** PCR test results (negative for WSSV, TSV, IHHNV, EMS/AHPND, EHP), and a health certificate if shipping interstate.
- **Acclimate carefully.** Drip acclimate PL over 1-2 hours to match your tank's temperature, salinity, and pH.

### Notes on Freshwater Prawns

- **Seasonality:** Juvenile production is seasonal in most US hatcheries (May-July). Plan ahead.
- **Stocking size:** Juveniles are typically 0.5-2 grams at stocking.
- **Grow-out period:** 4-6 months to market size in warm climates. Indoor heated systems extend the growing season.
- **Cannibalism:** Prawns are more territorial than shrimp. Provide substrate (bundles of netting, PVC, or artificial vegetation) to reduce aggression.

### Buying Tips for Ornamental Shrimp

- **Buy from breeders, not importers** when possible -- captive-bred stock adapts better to your water.
- **Start with Neocaridina** if you are new. They are hardy, breed readily, and tolerate a wide range of water parameters.
- **Buy in groups of 10+** to ensure a breeding colony and genetic diversity.
- **Quarantine new arrivals** for 2-4 weeks before adding to established colonies.
- **Shipping weather matters.** Avoid ordering during extreme heat (>95F) or cold (<30F) unless the seller uses insulated packaging with heat/cold packs.

## Buying Strategies for Backyard Growers

1. **Coordinate with other growers.** Split feed bags, PL orders, and bulk supply purchases to reduce per-unit cost and meet minimum orders.
2. **Buy used when appropriate.** IBC totes, stock tanks, and basic plumbing can be sourced used for significant savings. Never buy used aeration equipment -- rebuild kits are cheap and motors wear out.
3. **Start with what you need, not what you want.** A basic system (tote, Hakko pump, API test kit, Zeigler feed) can get you through your first grow-out for under $500 in equipment.
4. **Build relationships with suppliers.** Hatcheries and feed companies are more responsive to repeat customers. A phone call often gets better service than an email.
5. **Join online communities.** Facebook groups (Biofloc Shrimp Farming, Backyard Shrimp Farmers), forums, and Reddit (r/shrimptank, r/Aquariums) are excellent for finding deals, sourcing stock, and getting supplier reviews.
6. **Plan purchases seasonally.** Order PL in winter for spring delivery. Buy feed before grow-out season starts. Stock up on salt and minerals when prices are low.
