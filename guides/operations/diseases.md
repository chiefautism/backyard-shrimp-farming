# Disease Identification & Prevention

> Prevention is everything. Most shrimp diseases have no cure. Your best weapons: SPF stock, clean water, biosecurity, and probiotics.

## The Big Three: Vannamei Killers

### 1. White Spot Syndrome Virus (WSSV)

**The most devastating shrimp disease on Earth.**

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Type | Virus (Whispovirus) |
| Mortality | 100% within 3-10 days |
| Cure | **NONE** |
| Transmission | Contaminated water, equipment, birds, crabs, infected PLs |
| Temperature trigger | Often appears after cold snap (<25°C) |

**Symptoms**:
- White spots on carapace and shell (0.5-2mm diameter)
- Reddish discoloration of body and appendages
- Lethargy, gathering at tank edges
- Rapid reduction in feeding
- Swimming at surface
- Mass mortality starts 2-3 days after first symptoms

**Prevention**:
- Source **SPF post-larvae only**
- Filter incoming water (UV treatment if possible)
- Strict biosecurity: dedicated nets, buckets, hands washed between tanks
- Screen against birds and crabs (vectors)
- Avoid temperature drops below 25°C
- Quarantine any new additions

**If WSSV appears**:
1. Harvest anything salvageable immediately (cook thoroughly before eating - virus is not harmful to humans)
2. Cull remaining stock
3. Drain and disinfect tank completely
4. Disinfect ALL equipment (30 ppm chlorine for 30 minutes, then neutralize)
5. Dry tank in sun for several days
6. Start over with new SPF stock

### 2. Acute Hepatopancreatic Necrosis Disease (AHPND/EMS)

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Type | Bacterial (Vibrio parahaemolyticus with toxin plasmid) |
| Mortality | 40-100% in first 30 days after stocking |
| Cure | No reliable cure, management possible |
| Transmission | Contaminated water, biofilms, infected stock |

**Symptoms**:
- Pale, shrunken hepatopancreas (normally dark brown/orange)
- Empty stomach and midgut
- Soft shell
- Erratic swimming
- Rapid death of recently stocked PLs (first 20-30 days)
- Distinctive pale coloration

**Prevention**:
- Probiotics in water: Bacillus subtilis (10^6-10^8 CFU/ml)
- Avoid overfeeding (excess feed feeds Vibrio)
- Proper water quality maintenance
- SPF stock from reputable hatcheries
- Allow biofloc to establish before stocking (good bacteria outcompete Vibrio)

**Management**:
1. Reduce feeding by 50-70%
2. Increase aeration
3. Add probiotics (Bacillus-based products)
4. Partial water change if water quality is compromised
5. Garlic extract in feed (anti-Vibrio properties reported)

### 3. White Feces Disease (WFD)

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Type | Microsporidian (EHP - Enterocytozoon hepatopenaei) + Vibrio |
| Mortality | 20-60% if untreated |
| Cure | No definitive cure |
| Transmission | Contaminated PLs, water, equipment |

**Symptoms**:
- White fecal strings floating on water surface (distinctive sign)
- Loose shell
- Reduced growth
- Pale hepatopancreas
- Reduced feed consumption

**Prevention & Management**:
- Source EHP-free PLs (ask hatchery for PCR test results)
- Maintain strong biofloc/probiotic community
- Garlic extract in feed (1-2% inclusion rate)
- Reduce feeding
- Some farmers report success with herbal feed additives

## Other Food Shrimp Diseases

### Vibriosis

- **Cause**: Various Vibrio species (V. harveyi, V. alginolyticus, etc.)
- **Symptoms**: Luminescent bacteria on shell (glow in dark), black spots, shell erosion, lethargy
- **Prevention**: Good water quality, probiotics, avoid stress
- **Treatment**: Probiotics, improved conditions, remove affected individuals

### Black Gill Disease

- **Cause**: Accumulated particulate matter, fungi, bacteria
- **Symptoms**: Gills turn dark brown/black
- **Usually indicates**: Poor water quality (high organics, low DO)
- **Treatment**: Improve water quality, partial water changes, increase aeration

### Muscle Necrosis (White Tail Disease)

- **Cause**: Stress-induced (overcrowding, temperature extremes, handling)
- **Symptoms**: White opaque muscle tissue, especially in tail
- **Not infectious**: Stress response, not pathogen
- **Treatment**: Remove stressor, mild cases recover, severe cases don't

### Infectious Hypodermal and Hematopoietic Necrosis Virus (IHHNV)

- **Cause**: Virus
- **Symptoms**: Runt-deformity syndrome (stunted growth, deformed rostrum)
- **Not typically fatal** but reduces growth significantly
- **Prevention**: SPF stock

## Ornamental Shrimp Problems

### Vorticella

- **What**: Bell-shaped protozoan attached to shell/appendages
- **Looks like**: Fuzzy/fluffy coating on shrimp
- **Cause**: High organic load, poor water quality
- **Treatment**: Salt dip (1 tablespoon per liter, 30-60 seconds), improve water quality

### Scutariella japonica

- **What**: Parasitic flatworm/worm-like organism on head/rostrum
- **Looks like**: Tiny white worms around the head/antennae area
- **Treatment**: Salt dip (1 tbsp/L, 30-60 seconds), repeat in 7 days. Some use No Planaria.

### Ellobiopsidae (Green Fungus)

- **What**: Parasitic organism
- **Looks like**: Green/yellow fuzzy growth, usually on swimmerets (under tail)
- **Cause**: Introduced from wild-caught or contaminated stock
- **Treatment**: Remove infected shrimp, quarantine. No reliable in-tank treatment. Some report salt dips help.

### Planaria (Flatworms)

- **Not a disease** but a predator. Planaria eat baby shrimp.
- **Looks like**: Small flat white/brown worms that glide on surfaces
- **Cause**: Overfeeding (they thrive on excess food)
- **Treatment**: Planaria Zero (fenbendazole) - shrimp safe. Follow dosing instructions exactly. OR traps (meat in small bottle at night).

### Hydra

- **Not a disease** but a predator. Hydra catch and eat baby shrimp with stinging tentacles.
- **Looks like**: Tiny tentacled organisms attached to glass/plants
- **Treatment**: Fenbendazole (same as planaria treatment), manual removal, or brief heat treatment (raise tank to 40°C without shrimp)

## Prevention Protocol

### Biosecurity Checklist

- [ ] Source SPF/disease-free stock
- [ ] Quarantine new additions (separate tank, 2-4 weeks)
- [ ] Dedicated equipment per tank (nets, siphons, buckets)
- [ ] Wash hands between tanks
- [ ] Filter/treat incoming water
- [ ] Screen against birds and wild crustaceans (outdoor systems)
- [ ] Disinfect equipment: 30 ppm chlorine or 10% bleach solution, rinse thoroughly
- [ ] Never share water between tanks
- [ ] Never introduce wild-caught shrimp to your system

### Probiotic Protocol

| Product Type | Dosing | Frequency | Purpose |
|-------------|--------|-----------|---------|
| Bacillus subtilis | 10^6-10^8 CFU/ml in water | Weekly | Outcompetes Vibrio, water quality |
| Lactobacillus | Mix into feed (1-2%) | Daily | Gut health, immune boost |
| EM (Effective Microorganisms) | Per product label | Weekly | General water quality |
| Commercial (ProW, Sanolife) | Per product label | Per label | Combined probiotic formulations |

### Water Treatment for Incoming Water

| Method | What it Removes | Cost |
|--------|----------------|------|
| Chlorine neutralizer (Prime) | Chlorine, chloramine | $5/bottle |
| UV sterilizer | Bacteria, viruses, algae | $30-80 (one-time) |
| Activated carbon filtration | Chemicals, chlorine, organics | $10-20/filter |
| Settling (24-48 hours) | Suspended solids, some chlorine | Free |

## Diagnostic Flowchart

**Shrimp are dying. What now?**

```
START → Are they dying suddenly (hours) or gradually (days)?
│
├─ SUDDENLY (hours)
│   ├─ Check dissolved oxygen → LOW? → Emergency aeration, water change
│   ├─ Check temperature → Extreme? → Adjust heating/cooling
│   ├─ Check ammonia → HIGH? → Water change, stop feeding
│   └─ Check for chemical contamination (spray, soap, copper)
│
├─ GRADUALLY (days)
│   ├─ Are there white spots on shell? → WSSV → Cull, disinfect, start over
│   ├─ Are PLs dying within 30 days of stocking? → AHPND → Reduce feed, probiotics
│   ├─ White fecal strings floating? → WFD → Garlic in feed, probiotics
│   ├─ Shrimp turning red? → Severe stress or disease → Full parameter check
│   ├─ Soft shells? → Mineral deficiency → Test Ca/Mg/GH, supplement
│   ├─ Not eating? → Stress, pre-molt, or disease → Check parameters, observe
│   └─ Slow die-off, no obvious signs → Check ALL parameters, especially ammonia, nitrite, DO
│
└─ NONE DYING but looking sick
    ├─ Fuzzy coating → Vorticella → Salt dip, improve water quality
    ├─ White worms on head → Scutariella → Salt dip
    ├─ Green growth under tail → Ellobiopsidae → Remove individual
    ├─ Opaque white muscle → Stress → Remove stressor
    └─ Reduced activity/feeding → Check parameters, reduce feeding, observe
```
