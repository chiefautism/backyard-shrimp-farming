## Tax Considerations (USA)

### Hobby vs Business (IRS)

If you're making money, the IRS wants to know. The distinction:

| Factor | Hobby | Business |
|--------|-------|---------|
| Intent | Recreation | Profit |
| Tax form | Report on 1040 (limited deductions) | Schedule C |
| Deductions | Limited | Full business expenses |
| Losses | Cannot offset other income | Can offset other income (with limits) |
| IRS test | Not run as business | Run with profit intent, records, expertise |

**Rule of thumb**: If you profit in 3 of 5 years, the IRS generally accepts it as a business.

### Deductible Expenses (Schedule C)
- Equipment (tanks, pumps, heaters, test kits) - may depreciate over time
- Feed, salt, chemicals, supplements
- Electricity (portion used for farming)
- Water costs
- PLs/breeding stock
- Shipping supplies
- Vehicle mileage (deliveries, supply runs)
- Home office/space (if dedicated area used for farming)
- Education (books, courses, conferences)
- Insurance (if applicable)

**Keep receipts for everything. Use a spreadsheet or simple accounting software.**
